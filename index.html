Selected Projects

Easy Street
A smart shopping companion for the web.

Easy Street is a Chrome extension that transforms the online shopping experience by automatically identifying visually similar products, extracting details to filter and sort them on (i.e. price, size, color), and displaying them in a user-friendly side window. Through this, users are able to save both time and money, finding deals on products they would've otherwise never found. A sample image can be seen below. I designed the entire frontend user interface with React, and additionally worked on much of the backend extraction and standardization of product info. Easy Street is currently featured on the Chrome Web Store, and reaches triple-digit downloads a day.

Labyrinth
Navigate the world with your phone in your pocket. 

Labyrinth is an accessible navigation app for the 21st century. It works by calling the Google Maps API with the user's desired destination and method of transport, and it translates it into a series of coded vibrations that buzz whenever the user needs to make a turn. I created it to make navigation far more accessible to those who are hard of hearing and cannot afford to constantly check their phone while moving. Labyrinth ended up winning the 'Most Technical' award at IEEE's WI22 showcase.

Birds of a Feather
A social network for the classroom.

Birds of a Feather is a social networking app that connects students with other nearby students who they've shared classes with in the past. Returning to on-campus life for the first time after quarantine, we felt the need to create something that connected people. This app was developed with extensive Agile processes in a real-world environment, with me desiging the app's architecture and UI, managing project DevOps, and implementing the login and search systems.
